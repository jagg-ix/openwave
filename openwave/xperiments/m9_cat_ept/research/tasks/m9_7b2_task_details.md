# M9.7b2 task: coupled stationary radial Dirac--electrostatic solve

## Question

Does the selected 3D spherical nonlinear spinor model admit a nonzero,
normalizable stationary branch after its electrostatic field is solved from the
spinor density and fed back into the Dirac equation?

## Frozen ansatz

```text
Psi(r,t) = exp(-i omega t) (v(r) chi, i u(r) sigma.rhat chi)^T
rho = v^2 + u^2
S = v^2 - u^2.
```

The scored dimensionless equations are

```text
v' = -(omega - q phi + M) u
u' + 2u/r = (omega - q phi - M) v
M = m - lambda S
Q' = 4 pi r^2 q rho
phi' = -Q/(4 pi epsilon0 r^2).
```

## Frozen model inputs

```text
m = 1
epsilon0 = 1
lambda = 64
q = +1
integral 4 pi r^2 rho dr = 1.
```

The opposite dimensionless source sector is generated by `q -> -q`, which keeps
`q phi` unchanged while reversing `Q` and `phi`.

`lambda = 64` is a selected model input. A non-scoring exploratory solve was used
to locate this branch and set numerical tolerances. The result is not described
as blind preregistration and the coupling is not a CAT/EPT prediction.

## Boundary conditions

At the regular center:

```text
u(0) = 0
Q(0) = 0
N(0) = 0.
```

At the finite outer radius:

```text
N(R) = 1
phi(R) = Q(R)/(4 pi epsilon0 R)
u(R)/v(R) = (kappa + 1/R)/(m + omega - q phi(R))
kappa^2 = m^2 - (omega - q phi(R))^2.
```

The frequency is represented as `omega = m tanh(s)`, so every accepted numerical
solution lies inside the mass gap. Unit normalization excludes the trivial zero
solution.

## Solver

- SciPy adaptive fourth-order collocation BVP solver;
- electrostatic charge continuation through fractions `{0, 1/4, 1/2, 3/4, 1}`;
- real spherical upper and lower radial amplitudes;
- Coulomb-tail boundary condition at the finite window;
- independent finite-difference residual and energy diagnostics after convergence.

## Scored refinement

| Initial mesh | BVP tolerance | Window |
| ---: | ---: | ---: |
| 256 | `1e-5` | `R=40` |
| 512 | `2.5e-6` | `R=40` |
| 1024 | `6.25e-7` | `R=40` |

Successive spinor, density, and frequency differences must show observed order at
least three.

## Energy ledger

The matter energy is

```text
E_m = integral 4 pi r^2 [
  v(u' + 2u/r) - u v'
  + m(v^2-u^2)
  - lambda/2 (v^2-u^2)^2
] dr.
```

The electrostatic energy includes the exterior Coulomb tail:

```text
E_EM = integral_0^R Q(r)^2/(8 pi epsilon0 r^2) dr
       + Q(R)^2/(8 pi epsilon0 R).
```

It is checked independently against

```text
1/2 integral 4 pi r^2 q phi rho dr.
```

The stationary eigenvalue identity is checked from the Dirac equation rather than
assumed from the BVP boundary conditions.

## Additional gates

- fixed-spacing windows `R in {30,40,50}`;
- a fixed 5% opposite modulation of the initial upper/lower guess must converge to
  the same stationary branch;
- signed source-sector transformation;
- radial shell probability and the existing reflecting coarse-graining clock;
- core fraction inside `r <= 16` and outer-20% tail fraction.

The initial-guess modulation is a nonlinear solver-basin check. It is not a
time-dependent perturbation-stability test.

## Frozen acceptance

- all continuation stages converge and `0 < omega < m`;
- observed spinor, density, and frequency orders at least `3`;
- independent stationary residual at most `5e-7`;
- norm error at most `2e-9` and signed-charge error at most `1e-10`;
- relative Gauss residual at most `5e-6`;
- potential residual at most `1e-8` and boundary-flux error at most `1e-7`;
- field/source energy mismatch at most `1e-7`;
- stationary eigenvalue-identity error at most `5e-7`;
- core fraction at least `0.985`, outer-20% fraction at most `2e-4`;
- window frequency and central-potential spreads at most `1e-4`;
- window RMS-radius spread at most `5e-3`;
- the 5% initial-guess modulation returns within the declared branch tolerances;
- the radial information ledger contracts and closes.

## Scope boundary

Passing this task establishes a stationary solution of the selected classical
Soler--electrostatic model. It does not establish time-dependent stability,
magnetic or radiative Maxwell dynamics, calibrated electric charge, fermionic
quantization, an electron identity, or a CAT/EPT derivation of `lambda = 64`.

# M9.2 task: certify the free complex-field solver

## Question

Does the first M9 evolution engine reproduce the exact one-dimensional free
Gaussian packet while conserving the norm and energy of its own discrete
Hamiltonian?

This task certifies numerical infrastructure. It is not a particle-existence
search and it introduces no nonlinear CAT/EPT term.

## Frozen equation and method

```text
i hbar d_t psi = -(hbar^2 / 2m) d_xx psi
```

The discretization is:

- periodic domain `[-20, 20)`;
- second-order centered finite-difference Laplacian;
- Crank--Nicolson time update;
- `dt = 0.1 dx`, adjusted only so the final step lands exactly at `t = 2`;
- refinement levels `N = 128, 256, 512`.

Crank--Nicolson uses the same discrete Laplacian in the update and energy ledger.
Therefore norm and discrete energy conservation are tested separately from
convergence to the continuum solution.

## Frozen Gaussian packet

```text
sigma = 1
x0 = -4
k0 = 1.5
m = 1
hbar = 1
```

The exact continuum energy is

```text
E = hbar^2/(2m) * (k0^2 + 1/(4 sigma^2)) = 1.25.
```

## Acceptance criteria

All must pass:

1. maximum norm drift across the three levels `<= 1e-12`;
2. maximum discrete-energy drift `<= 1e-12`;
3. observed order at least `1.8` for phase-aligned wave-function L2 error;
4. observed order at least `1.8` for density L1 error;
5. observed order at least `1.8` for current relative L2 error;
6. finest density L1 error `<= 1e-2`;
7. finest current relative L2 error `<= 1e-2`;
8. finest overlap fidelity `>= 0.9999`;
9. finest overlap phase error `<= 1e-2` radians;
10. finest continuum-energy relative error `<= 5e-3`;
11. finest probability in the outer 20 percent of the periodic box `<= 1e-12`.

The boundary criterion prevents a passing result from being generated by a
packet that has wrapped around the periodic domain.

## Failure interpretation

Failure would reject this discretization or parameter regime as the M9 base
solver. Passing does not establish localization: the exact free packet spreads,
so M9.2 is deliberately a control case for the later nonlinear decision gate.

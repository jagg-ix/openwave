## 3. The stability criteria the read imports

Vakhitov-Kolokolov (1973): spectral stability decided by the slope dQ/dω (standard convention dQ/dω < 0 on the stable branch). Grillakis-Shatah-Strauss (1987): orbital stability from convexity of d(ω) = E − ωQ, d''(ω) = −dQ/dω. For TOPOLOGICALLY stabilized solitons under isorotation, the operative bounds are instead: (i) deformation / symmetry breaking above a critical ω (rigid-body kin constancy fails first), (ii) the RADIATION window: stable isospinning states exist only for ω below the lightest propagating mode mass (Battye-Haberichter: ω ≤ min(μ, 1)); beyond it the soliton sheds J as radiation, and rigid-ansatz solutions past the window are artefacts, (iii) non-radiation as the organizing condition for all stationary spinning solitons (Radu-Volkov Phys. Rep. 468).

## 6. Honest limits of the mapping (kept from the arms)

Gradient descent is not the Hamiltonian flow (descent divergence proves E unbounded below; fatality under true dynamics depends on energy exchange, which conserved E + fixed J can forbid). Fixed-J stability is conditional: anything that changes J (external coupling, dissipation) reopens the negative direction. GR's constraints are infinitely many first-class gauge generators; our J is ONE global charge, so the GR analogy is structural, not quantitative. The quantum vacuum-decay bounds assume a Lorentz-invariant QFT vacuum; a classical substrate sits outside those assumptions.
